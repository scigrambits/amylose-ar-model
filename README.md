### Biological Significance

Amylose serves as an important energy storage molecule in plants, particularly in seeds, tubers, and roots. Its linear structure makes it more digestible than highly branched amylopectin, though it is also more resistant to enzymatic breakdown when in its crystalline form (resistant starch).

### Applications

- **Food industry**: Affects texture, digestibility, and gel-forming properties of starch-based foods
- **Bioplastics**: Used in biodegradable packaging materials
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Employed as excipients and in controlled drug delivery systems
- **Industrial uses**: Applied in paper manufacturing, adhesives, and textile processing
